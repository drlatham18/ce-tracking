# Tennessee LPC Continuing Education Requirements

Reference summary for the Tennessee Board of Licensed Professional Counselors, Marital and Family Therapists, and Clinical Pastoral Therapists. Last updated 2026-05-31. **Verify directly with the board before relying on any figure marked [verify with TN board].**

## Authoritative source on file

`ce/sources/TN-Board-Policy-Statement-on-CE-90-day-grace-period_2012-06-29.pdf` — Board Policy Statement on Continuing Education, originally adopted **March 4, 2011**, amended **June 29, 2012**. The hour totals and disciplinary procedure below are taken directly from that policy.

## Core requirements (per Board Policy Statement, amended 2012-06-29)

- **Hours per calendar year:** The policy is **annual**, not biennial.
  - 10 clock hours per calendar year — single license/certificate from this Board.
  - 15 clock hours per calendar year — two licenses/certificates from this Board.
  - 20 clock hours per calendar year — three licenses/certificates from this Board.
- **Practice-related minimum:** At least **5 clock hours** must be directly related to the practice of *each* profession for which the person is licensed or certified.
- **First-year exemption:** A licensee is exempt from CE requirements for the calendar year in which he/she is licensed.
- **Underlying rules:** Rule 0450-1-.12 (LPC), 0450-2-.12 (MFT), 0450-3-.12 (CPT) — consult these for the full CE requirements that the policy statement references.

## Grace period and disciplinary procedure (from the 2012 policy)

If the Board or its staff discovers the licensee has failed to obtain CE in a timely manner:

1. **Advisory Censure (informal) — 90 days.** Letter is issued; licensee has 90 days from the letter date to make up the deficient hours and demonstrate compliance. No formal discipline on the record at this stage.
2. **Deficiency letter + Agreed Citation ($200) — additional 180 days.** If the 90-day window passes without compliance, the Board issues a deficiency letter and a $200 Agreed Citation (offer of settlement). Licensee has 180 days from that letter to complete deficient hours and demonstrate compliance. The $200 civil penalty is **formal discipline**, reportable on the Department's Disciplinary Action Report (DAR) and noted on the licensure profile. Licensee may accept the citation or request a contested case before the Board.
3. **Formal disciplinary action.** If the licensee fails to both pay the $200 and complete the hours within the 180-day window, the Tennessee Department of Health Office of General Counsel files formal disciplinary action — possible outcomes include additional penalties, costs, and disciplinary action up to and including license revocation.

## Items from secondary sources (may reflect rule updates the 2012 policy statement does not capture)

These items appeared in secondary CE-provider summaries retrieved 2026-05-31 and do not appear in the 2012 Board policy on file. They may reflect later rule revisions to 0450-1-.12, or they may be wrong — **[verify with TN board]** before planning around any of them.

- **Possible biennial 20-hour figure.** Some secondary sources describe a 20-hour-per-renewal-cycle (biennial) structure with deadlines tied to the licensee's birth month. The 2012 Board policy on file is unambiguously annual (10/15/20 per calendar year by license count). The renewal-cycle figure may reflect a more recent rule revision, or it may be a misreading of the per-calendar-year requirement aggregated across the 2-year renewal window. **[verify with TN board]**
- **Ethics / TN law / board rules:** Secondary sources cite a 3-hour minimum in ethics, TCA Title 63 Ch. 22, or Compilation Ch. 0450-01. **[verify with TN board]** — not in the 2012 policy on file.
- **Suicide prevention:** Secondary sources cite a 2-hour board-approved suicide prevention training requirement every 4 years. **[verify with TN board]** — not in the 2012 policy on file.
- **Supervision-specific CE for supervisors:** Secondary sources cite a 3-hour supervision-specific CE requirement for licensees who hold supervisor status. **[verify with TN board]** — not in the 2012 policy on file.
- **Asynchronous on-demand cap:** Secondary sources cite a 10-hour-per-cycle hard cap on async on-demand training. **[verify with TN board]** — not in the 2012 policy on file.
- **Live synchronous virtual CE:** Status unclear in secondary sources; the rule was reportedly under revision. **[verify with TN board]** before relying on an all-virtual plan.
- **Documentation retention:** Secondary sources suggest retaining CE documentation for 4 years for audit. **[verify with TN board]** — not in the 2012 policy on file.
